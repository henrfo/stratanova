About Strata Nova

Strata Nova is a space for exploring how neuroscience, machine learning, and human experience come together. It’s a project in motion — shaped by study, research, and curiosity.

Rather than waiting until ideas are fully formed or formalized, Strata Nova is about learning in the open. It's a place to think, share, and build — while still figuring things out. The goal is to connect knowledge across disciplines, and to invite others into that process over time.

It began as a student-led initiative, but it’s not limited to that. Strata Nova reflects a belief that learning and doing don’t have to be separate — that research, design, and insight can grow side by side, even in early stages.

At its core, it’s about staying curious — and making that curiosity visible.

Henrik

henrik@stratanova.no

I'm a psychology and computer science student at the University of Oslo, with a background in Norwegian film.

My research interests lie at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience, neuroimaging, machine learning, and data science. I’m particularly interested in vision, perception, memory, decision-making, and predictive modeling, using statistical and computational methods to study brain dynamics.

In addition to Norway, my exchange studies in Japan, Australia, and the US have given me a broader perspective on different approaches to these questions.

At the core of my work is an interest in how psychology, neuroscience, and technology come together to deepen our understanding of cognition, behavior and human interaction with the world.
