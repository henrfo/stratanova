Exploring Biomarkers of Cognitive Training Responsiveness After Stroke

Bachelor Thesis: An exploratory multimodal machine learning approach examining potential relationships between brain features and cognitive training outcomes in stroke patients, integrating structural MRI, blood biomarkers, and normative deviation metrics.

R² = 0.22 (bootstrap) In-sample Performance
n = 54 Stroke Patients
n = 951 Healthy Controls

Our Approach

We conducted an exploratory in-sample analysis using XGBoost and SHAP to identify potential biological correlates:

  • Structural T1-weighted MRI features
  • Blood biomarkers
  • Structural covariance patterns (PCA-derived)
  • Autoencoder-derived normative deviation metrics comparing stroke patients to healthy brain structure

Research Implications

This hypothesis-generating study identified biologically plausible relationships between distributed neural systems and cognitive training responsiveness. The findings suggest potential directions for future research with larger samples that could eventually inform more personalized rehabilitation approaches.
