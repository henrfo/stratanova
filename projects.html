Predicting Cognitive Training Gain After Stroke

A multimodal machine learning approach that predicts which stroke patients will benefit most from cognitive rehabilitation, combining structural MRI, inflammatory biomarkers, and advanced deviation metrics.

R² = 0.42-0.49 Predictive Performance
n = 54 Stroke Patients
n = 900 Healthy Controls

Our Approach

We developed an XGBoost model that integrates multiple data types to predict cognitive training outcomes:

  • Structural T1-weighted MRI features
  • Inflammatory blood markers
  • 20 PCA-derived components (from approximately 1200 T1 MRI features)
  • 20 autoencoder-based brain deviation metrics (plus reconstruction error + Mahalanobis distance)

Why It Matters

By accurately predicting which patients will benefit most from cognitive training, this approach improves understanding of features that are important for post-stroke recovery, which if validated on a larger sample, may contribute to more personalized rehabilitation strategies
